# Curious Mind — Brand Guidelines
*Source: Riktlinjer för varumärkesidentitet v1.0 (last updated 2023-03-29)*

## 01 Strategy

### Brand idea
**Säg mer. / Say more.**

Our purpose is to simplify, clarify, and distil messages to the most essential so that people understand and can act on them. The focus is not beautiful adjectives or melodic copy — it's about making it clear for the recipient. We choose what to leave out.

### Position
**Din contentpartner. / Your content partner.**

### Values
| Value | Swedish | Descriptors |
|---|---|---|
| Warm | Varma | Empathetic, mindful, passionate |
| Innovative | Nytänkande | Motivated, methodical, forward-thinking |
| Self-confident | Självsäkra | Confident, challenging, versatile |

### Brand platform (pyramid)
- **Top — Brand message:** Säg mer
- **Middle — Position:** Din contentpartner
- **Base — Values:** Varma · Nytänkande · Självsäkra

### Brand story
One of our greatest antagonists is noise. Together, we're exposed to six to ten thousand messages per day. Constant overload of impressions, options, and endless scrolling makes creating and reaching people with communication harder than ever.

Say more, not more. It's about distilling messages to the most essential so that people understand and can act on them. We make communication that simplifies and clarifies. We are their content partner and we make communication that says more.

## 02 Logo

### Concept
The logo is our strongest brand identifier. In its basic form it consists of a symbol and a logotype that together form an image that represents our brand. The logo's distinct elements make a consistent expression possible while offering flexibility to adapt to different formats.

### Components
- **Symbol** — the icon mark alone (the abstract geometric form derived from the letter C, with two rounded shapes)
- **Logotype** — "Curious Mind" wordmark in Foundry Unie
- **Combined** — symbol + logotype (primary usage)

### Logo variants
- **Horizontal** — symbol left, logotype right (primary)
- **Stacked** — symbol above, logotype below
- **Symbol only** — for contexts where the full logotype doesn't fit

### Approved background colours
The logo works on:
- White / light backgrounds
- Black / dark backgrounds
- Orange (brand primary)
- Yellow (brand secondary)

The symbol uses reversed (white) treatment on dark and coloured backgrounds.

### Rules & restrictions
- Minimum clear space must be maintained around the logo at all times
- Do not distort, rotate, recolour, or add effects to the logo
- Do not place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Always use approved versions from the asset library

## 03 Typography

### Primary typeface: Foundry Unie
A clear, simple, and functional typeface with geometric character traits. It ties to our brand and is used in both print and digital media.

**Weights in use:**
| Weight | Style |
|---|---|
| Regular | Body text, captions |
| Regular Italics | Emphasis within body |
| Demibold | Headlines, subheadings |

**Usage:**
- Headlines: left-aligned, large scale (e.g. 70pt for primary headlines)
- Body text: left-aligned, functional and readable
- Hierarchical system: large headline → sub-headline → body text

### Secondary typeface: Arial
Used when Foundry Unie is not available (everyday documents, presentations, emails, etc.). Arial is the accessibility fallback across all platforms.

**Weights in use:**
| Weight | Style |
|---|---|
| Regular | Body text |
| Regular Italics | Emphasis |
| Bold | Headlines, subheadings |

### Layout principles
- Typography is always **left-aligned** (no centred or right-aligned text)
- Headlines at 70pt (or equivalent large scale) for key visual statements
- Grid-based layout — content is placed in a structured, functional grid
- The typeface works both informationally and visually as a graphic element

## 04 Colours

### Overview
Colour is a strong carrier of the Curious Mind brand. The palette is warm, with orange as the driving, recognition-creating primary colour across all channels. Combined with yellows, it broadens expression. Mixed with neutral warm greys and whites it creates a broad but well-defined palette suitable for a wide range of applications.

### Full colour palette

| Colour name | HEX | RGB | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Orange** | `#FF754B` | 255, 117, 75 | Primary brand colour — driving, recognition-creating across all channels |
| **Varm gul** (Warm yellow) | `#FFAC4A` | 255, 172, 74 | Secondary warm — broadens the palette alongside orange |
| **Gul** (Yellow) | `#FFDA56` | 255, 218, 86 | Tertiary warm — lighter, brighter complement |
| **Lila** (Purple) | `#8320FF` | 131, 32, 255 | Accent — high contrast; used for labels, tags, interactive elements |
| **Svart** (Black) | `#000000` | 0, 0, 0 | Neutral dark — primary text and dark backgrounds |
| **Grå** (Grey) | `#D0D0D0` | 208, 208, 208 | Neutral mid — dividers, secondary surfaces |
| **Ljus grå** (Light grey) | `#F0F0F0` | 240, 240, 240 | Neutral light — background surfaces |
| **Vit** (White) | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | Neutral lightest — primary content background |

### Primary colours in practice
Orange is established as the strongest brand colour and should create recognition across all channels. It functions in all contexts — including small sizes and margins — with a focus on being placed where it creates the most energy.

### Complementary colour usage
When expanding the Curious Mind palette, complementary colours add energy and warmth. The focus remains on orange, with complementary colours serving a supporting role.

### Colour + typography
Text set on primary colour backgrounds must maintain readability. Approved combinations:
- Black text on orange / yellow / white
- White text on black
- Specific contrast ratios are defined in the digital palette

## 05 Photography (Bildspråk)

### Purpose
Photography plays an important role in visualising how Curious Mind operates in everyday life and how our employees communicate with each other. It exists in two categories.

### Category 1: Portrait photos (Porträttbilder)
- Shot in a **studio setting** to convey a professional and sophisticated feel
- The photographer uses a slight blur — the lens does not point directly at the person but slightly to the side, giving depth to the image
- Employees are photographed against a **light grey background** for a clean, modern look
- Ensures consistency: new portraits share the same style

**Key qualities:** Professional, controlled, consistent, clean.

### Category 2: Personal / candid photos (Personliga bilder)
- Not about perfect, staged images — about capturing **genuine, shared moments**
- Shot on location at the office or in a shared space such as a lounge or meeting room
- The most important thing is that the photographer captures how the company works in everyday life — how employees interact with each other
- Natural light is preferred; lighting should have the **same light quality** as portrait photos for consistency

**Key qualities:** Authentic, unposed, relatable, warm.

### General photography principles
- Consistent light quality across both categories
- Natural and warm, not cold or clinical
- Avoid stock photography aesthetics — images should feel genuinely Curious Mind
- Images used in channels and applications should have a focus on people

## 06 The Fifth Element (Femte element)

### Concept
The fifth element is rooted in the geometric design of Curious Mind's symbol, interpreted in abstract graphic forms. Both decorative and identity-bearing, it gives us the possibility to create a living, high-quality visual expression that represents our brand. It can be used as a **decorative element** and also as an **identity carrier**.

### Form
- **3D rendered organic orb/blob shapes** — spherical and fluid, referencing the rounded forms of the logo symbol
- Can be used as a single animated version or in free compositions
- Multiple shapes can be combined freely for compositions

### Colour variants
The fifth element exists in three colour families:

| Variant | Description |
|---|---|
| **Orange** | Primary — warm orange, ties directly to the brand primary colour |
| **Varm gul** (Warm yellow) | Secondary — warm golden yellow |
| **Gul** (Yellow) | Tertiary — lighter, brighter yellow |

Each colour variant has multiple compositions (numbered versions 01–06+) to choose from.

### Usage
- Can be used across all channels and applications
- Can be static or animated
- Scales to different formats — from large hero visuals to small decorative details
- Should not be overused; functions best as a focal point or complementary visual energy
